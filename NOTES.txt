NOTES — Solectrac CAN-bus reverse-engineering findings
======================================================

These notes record what we learned about *this specific tractor* and *these
specific captures*. The README covers the parser itself in generic terms;
this file is where the experimental, observational, and tentative material
lives. Numbers below come from the captures in this directory and are
subject to revision when better data or vendor documentation arrives.


Tractor / pack context
----------------------

* Tractor: Solectrac electric tractor (~70 V class).
* Pack chemistry inferred from cell voltages: NMC (not LFP).
    - Resting cells in the ~3.65 V range at ~40% reported SOC matches a
      partial-discharge NMC OCV curve, not LFP's flat plateau.
* Pack topology: 20 series cells (s = 20). Module-temperature frames carry
  enough channels for several thermistors per module; 8 channels per frame
  across F155..F15E means the BMS reserves space for up to 80 channels even
  though far fewer are actually populated on this pack.
* SOC at time of capture: ~40% (operator-reported).
* Ambient: ~55 °F (~13 °C); tractor was outdoors.
* The PTO/drive inverter and motor controller do **not** appear on the bus
  we logged. The diagnostic port we tapped is the BMS/charger CAN bus
  (sometimes called the "accessory" or "vehicle" bus). The dashboard is
  almost certainly a dual-bus gateway between this bus and the drivetrain
  bus, which would explain how it can show RPM/temps/SOC/etc. without all
  of those signals being visible here.


Captures used
-------------

* `continuous.csv`  — long resting baseline at ~40% SOC. Useful for noise
                      floor, standby draw, and temperature stability.
* `startingup.csv`  — wake-up transition. Vehicle-controller heartbeat
                      transitions from 0x00 (init) to 0x0C (ready).
* `driving.csv`     — ~30 s of normal driving with no implements.
* `dph.csv`         — driving *plus hydraulics*. Same drive activity as
                      `driving.csv` with the hydraulic circuit active in
                      addition. Despite the name "driving plus hydraulics"
                      this capture also contains substantial idle/parked
                      time that drags its mean current down — compare
                      *peak* / high-percentile values, not means, when
                      contrasting it with `driving.csv`.
* `charging.csv`    — charging from ~40% SOC.
* `data.csv`        — *excluded*. Contains additional injected/unrelated
                      OBD-II "Tester Present" traffic the operator was
                      generating at the same time. Not part of the BMS/
                      charger conversation we're trying to characterize.


Source-address (SA) map observed in these captures
--------------------------------------------------

| SA   | Role inferred                           | Notes                          |
|------|------------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| 0xF3 | BMS                                      | Owns the F100/F102/F104/F106/  |
|      |                                          | F107/F108/F113../F155.. set.   |
| 0xE5 | On-board charger                         | Talks FF50 telemetry.          |
| 0xF4 | Vehicle controller (drive-side)          | Sends 1806E5F4 (request to     |
|      |                                          | charger SA 0xE5).              |

|      |                                          | byte-0 transitions 0x00 → 0x0C |

| 0x41 | Address-claim type frame                 | Few frames; not analyzed.      |


PGN decode notes (BMS, SA = 0xF3)
---------------------------------

All scalings below were derived empirically. Where a scaling is consistent
with sane physical units across multiple captures it's marked CONFIRMED;
otherwise it's TENTATIVE.

F113..F13C — "BMS cell voltages N..N+3"
    8 bytes = 4 × big-endian uint16 in millivolts.
    F113 = cells 0..3, F114 = cells 4..7, ... F13C = cells 164..167.
    Only the first 20 cells of F113..F117 have nonzero / sensible
    values for this pack; the rest are 0xFFFF (sentinel) or 0.
    CONFIRMED: BE u16 mV. Cells read ~3.6–3.7 V at ~40% SOC.

F155..F15E — "BMS module temps 8N..8N+7"
    8 bytes = 8 × uint8 with J1939 +40 °C offset (i.e. raw 53 = 13 °C).
    F155 = channels 0..7, F156 = 8..15, ... F15E = 72..79.
    Most channels are 0xFF (not present); a small number reflect ambient
    and were what let us pin down the +40 °C convention.
    CONFIRMED: u8, offset 40 °C.

F102F3 — "BMS cell min/max summary"
    Carries pack-wide cell statistics. We use:
      * a max-cell mV (BE u16)
      * a min-cell mV (BE u16)
      * spread = max - min (mV)
      * a byte that appears to encode the index of the lowest cell
      * status/flag bits in another byte
      * a coarse pack-voltage estimate (sum of 20 × cell average), kept
        as a sanity check
    CONFIRMED for max/min/spread; index/flag byte assignments are
    TENTATIVE — they are stable but their *labels* are guessed.

F100F3 — "BMS pack status (incl. |I|)"
    * Bytes 2..3: a 16-bit field that scales like a pack-voltage proxy.
      Magnitude is plausible but the exact LSB is TENTATIVE.
    * Byte 4: pack-current magnitude at 0.1 A/bit — this is the |I|
      reading the script labels as "pack current". CONFIRMED for
      magnitude (matches charger current during charging within
      rounding); the *sign* (charge vs discharge) is **not** carried in
      this byte. Direction must be inferred from context (vehicle state,
      charger active, etc.) until we identify the sign bit.
    * Other bytes likely include SOC/SOH/temperature summaries; not yet
      decoded.

F104F3 — "BMS temp min/max summary"
    Pack-wide hottest/coldest module-temperature summary, analogous to
    F102 for cells. Not currently parsed into its own CSV.

F106F3 — "BMS state (charger-dependent)"
    Periodic frame whose contents differ between idle, driving, and
    charging captures. Likely BMS state machine + flags. Not decoded.

F107F3 — "BMS current/voltage limits"
    Looks like the standard J1939 BMS "limits" frame (charge current
    limit, discharge current limit, voltage limits). Bytes are stable
    when SOC is stable, change near limits. Not decoded.

F108F3 — "BMS aux status"
    Auxiliary/contactor/heater status flags. Not decoded.


PGN decode notes (charger, SA = 0xE5)
-------------------------------------

FF50E5 — "Charger telemetry (V, A)"
    Proprietary B frame from the on-board charger.
      * byte 0 = status / mode (0 when idle, nonzero when charging)
      * bytes 1..2 (LE u16) = output voltage at ~1/3 V/bit
        (raw 0x00DC ≈ 73.3 V matches the pack at the time)
      * bytes 3..4 (LE u16) = output current at 0.1 A/bit
    CONFIRMED for V and A magnitudes during charging; status byte
    semantics (beyond "0 = idle") are TENTATIVE.


PGN decode notes (vehicle controller, SA = 0xD0)
------------------------------------------------

F100D0 — "VC heartbeat"
    Note: same PGN (F100) as the BMS pack-status frame, but a different
    *source*. The PGN namespace is global; it's the SA that disambiguates.
    Observed byte-0 values:
      * 0x00 — "init" (early in startingup.csv, before key/wake completes)
      * 0x0C — "ready" (steady state once tractor is awake)
    The transition is sharp and aligns with the operator-described
    wake-up event, which is what gives us confidence in the labels.
    Other bytes change but haven't been decoded.


Confirmed quantitative findings
-------------------------------

* Resting / standby pack draw (continuous.csv): on the order of
  100–200 W. Consistent with BMS + dash + accessory loads, no traction.
* Driving (driving.csv): peak draw on the order of ~1 kW; typical
  driving current well under nameplate.
* Driving + hydraulics (dph.csv): hydraulic actuation adds clearly
  visible current spikes on top of the drive baseline; peak combined
  draw on the order of ~1.8 kW. The mean of dph.csv is *lower* than
  driving.csv only because dph.csv includes more idle/parked time —
  per-event peaks are higher in dph.
* Charging (charging.csv): charger output on the order of ~1.5 kW into
  the pack at this SOC. Pack |I| reported by F100 byte 4 matches
  charger current from FF50E5 within rounding, which is what cross-
  validates the 0.1 A/bit scaling.
* Cell spread at rest: tens of mV. No outlier cells observed.
* No active DM1 trouble codes during any capture (FECA frames are
  emitted but their SPN/FMI fields are inactive).
* Temperatures: all populated channels read 12–15 °C, consistent with
  the ~13 °C ambient. No self-heating signature in any capture (none
  of these runs are long or hard enough to warm the pack).


Open questions / future work
----------------------------

* F100 bytes 2..3 voltage scaling — the LSB is TENTATIVE. Capturing a
  full charge from low SOC to 100% should let us pin it down by fitting
  to the known charger voltage from FF50.
* Pack-current sign — F100 byte 4 is magnitude only. Look for a sign
  bit elsewhere in F100 (possibly in the status/flags byte) or for
  sign in F107/F108.
* F106 / F107 / F108 byte-level decoding — not yet attempted. Compare
  values across charging-vs-idle-vs-driving captures to isolate which
  byte is which.
* Drivetrain bus — find the second CAN connector. The dashboard
  receives RPM/motor telemetry that is *not* on this bus, so there is
  another CAN segment somewhere on the vehicle. Plausible options:
    - a separate diagnostic port (look for one near the controller)
    - a 2-pin service tap (small Deutsch connector)
    - a backshell tap on the dash harness
  Once tapped, expect to see SAE-standard PGNs like 61444 (EEC1, RPM),
  65262 (Engine Temperature), and similar.
* Full-SOC capture — operator agreed to capture a continuous file at
  100% SOC. That data should refine the cell-voltage range and
  validate the pack-voltage scaling.
* Confirm whether dashboard is the bus gateway, or whether a dedicated
  gateway ECU sits between the two segments. Either is plausible.


Confidence summary
------------------

CONFIRMED:
    cell-voltage scaling (BE u16 mV)
    module-temp scaling (u8 + 40 °C)
    pack-current magnitude (F100 byte 4 @ 0.1 A/bit)
    charger output V (FF50 LE u16 @ 1/3 V/bit)
    charger output A (FF50 LE u16 @ 0.1 A/bit)
    vehicle-controller heartbeat init/ready states (F100D0 byte 0)

TENTATIVE / GUESSED:
    F102 spread-related index/flag byte assignments
    F100 voltage scaling (bytes 2..3)
    FF50 status-byte semantics beyond 0/nonzero
    All of F106 / F107 / F108

UNKNOWN:
    pack-current sign
    SA 0x12 / 0xCA roles beyond the surface descriptions above
    everything on the (un-tapped) drivetrain bus
