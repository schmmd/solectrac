NOTES — Solectrac CAN-bus reverse-engineering findings
======================================================

These notes record what we learned about *this specific tractor* and *these
specific captures*. The README covers the parser itself in generic terms;
this file is where the experimental, observational, and tentative material
lives. Numbers below come from the captures in this directory and are
subject to revision when better data or vendor documentation arrives.


Tractor / pack context
----------------------

* Tractor: Solectrac electric tractor (~70 V class).
* Pack chemistry: NMC (vendor screenshot labels it "NiCoMn"), confirming
  what the OCV-curve shape already suggested.
* Pack topology: **20 series cells** (s = 20), now CONFIRMED both from
  per-cell PGN coverage and from the vendor BMS GUI ("Cells in series: 20",
  "Online cell count: 20"). Module-temperature frames reserve up to 80
  channels across F155..F15E but the pack has **7 temperature probes**
  ("Temp. probe count: 7" / "Online temp. count: 7" in the GUI) — anything
  beyond the first 7 channels in `temps.csv` is padding/noise.
* Pack capacity / ratings (from the GUI, not derivable from CAN alone):
    - Rated capacity: 300 Ah
    - Rated total voltage: 72.0 V nominal
    - Rated discharge current: 300 A
    - Rated DC charge: 78 A
    - Rated AC charge: 39 A
* SOC at time of capture: ~40% (operator-reported) for the original
  recorded-data/*.csv set, ~100% for the asc/*.asc set.
* Ambient: ~55 °F (~13 °C); tractor was outdoors.
* Drive-train state: most asc/*.asc captures were taken with the tractor
  in **neutral**. "Full throttle" in those filenames refers to throttle
  pedal position, not actual mechanical load on the wheels. The
  loader-arms.asc / loader2.asc captures (2026-05-09) added hydraulic
  load with the motor controller silent — together with driving.asc
  these were enough to nail down the F100F3 pack-current encoding
  (see the F100F3 section).
* The motor controller / drive ECU **is** on this bus after all (SA 0xCA),
  it just doesn't speak standard J1939 EEC1. Its telemetry is carried in
  the proprietary B frame FF21CA — see the FF21CA decode section below.
  This was confirmed empirically: accellerate-decelerate.asc produces a
  clean 0 → ~2500 → 0 RPM trace from FF21CA bytes 3..4 LE that matches
  the operator's reported reading of "near 2500" at full throttle.

  Whether there is *also* a separate drivetrain bus carrying the
  inverter / PTO is still open. The dashboard could be reading RPM
  directly from FF21CA on this bus, or it could be a dual-bus gateway
  with FF21CA being a re-broadcast. Either way, the most operator-
  visible drive-side data is reachable from here.


Captures used
-------------

* `continuous.csv`  — long resting baseline at ~40% SOC. Useful for noise
                      floor, standby draw, and temperature stability.
* `startingup.csv`  — wake-up transition. Vehicle-controller heartbeat
                      transitions from 0x00 (init) to 0x0C (ready).
* `driving.csv`     — ~30 s of normal driving with no implements.
* `dph.csv`         — driving *plus hydraulics*. Same drive activity as
                      `driving.csv` with the hydraulic circuit active in
                      addition. Despite the name "driving plus hydraulics"
                      this capture also contains substantial idle/parked
                      time that drags its mean current down — compare
                      *peak* / high-percentile values, not means, when
                      contrasting it with `driving.csv`.
* `charging.csv`    — charging from ~40% SOC.
* `data.csv`        — *excluded*. Contains additional injected/unrelated
                      OBD-II "Tester Present" traffic the operator was
                      generating at the same time. Not part of the BMS/
                      charger conversation we're trying to characterize.

A second batch of captures (Vector ASC format, in `asc/`), taken at
~100% SOC with the tractor in neutral, is also referenced below:

* `normal-ignition.asc`           — wake-up baseline, no charger.
* `ignition-with-charger-inserted.asc` — wake-up with charger plug
                                          inserted in the tractor's
                                          charge port BUT no AC power
                                          to the charger. Useful for
                                          isolating "charger detected"
                                          flags vs. "actively charging".
* `full-throttle.asc`             — throttle pedal pressed, in neutral.
* `full-throttle-hydraulics.asc`  — full throttle plus hydraulics
                                     actuated; tractor still in neutral.
* `accellerate-decelerate.asc`    — varying throttle, in neutral.

Unlike the recorded-data/*.csv captures, the ASC files carry real
microsecond-resolution per-frame timestamps, which unblocks all
time-domain analysis (rate, dV/dt, integrated current, etc.).

A third batch of captures (top-level `amp-*.asc`, 2026-05-09) provides
operator-confirmed dashboard-amperage ground truth for the F100F3 pack-
current decode. Each filename's number is the approximate dashboard-
displayed current at the time of recording:

* `amp-1.asc`   — dashboard ~1 A   (true idle, in neutral)
* `amp-18.asc`  — dashboard ~18 A
* `amp-35.asc`  — dashboard ~35 A
* `amp-42.asc`  — dashboard ~42 A
* `amp-58.asc`  — dashboard ~58 A

These were the captures that finally pinned down the high-byte / low-
byte rollover behavior across the 25.5 / 51.1 / 76.7 A boundaries, and
are now the cleanest cross-validation set for the F100F3 decode (see
the F100F3 section below).

A charging trace (`asc/charging-120V-90ish-to-100.asc`, 2026-05-09)
provides the first capture with the charger actively delivering power
to the pack, taken from ~90 % SOC on a 120 V outlet:

* `charging-120V-90ish-to-100.asc` — 2863/3007 active-charging frames
                                       (FF50 status = 0x03). End-to-end
                                       it shows a textbook CC→CV taper
                                       (~18.5 A → ~9.9 A → ~2.9 A) at
                                       constant ~83 V. This is what
                                       finally anchored the FF50
                                       voltage and current scales (see
                                       the FF50E5 section).

A 100%-SOC idle capture with the charger plug inserted but no real
charging happening (BMS done topping off):

* `soc-100-idle.asc`                — charger present, current near
                                       zero. Useful as the "plugged in,
                                       BMS done" reference point.

Fault-bitmap captures (`asc/bms-error-codes/`, 2026-05-09) gave us a
matched-mode pair that nailed down F108 as the BMS active-fault bitmap:

* `bms-124-140-142-143-144-146.asc` — operator confirmed the dashboard
                                       was cycling through codes 124,
                                       140, 142, 143, 144, 146 (each
                                       held on screen ~1 s) for the
                                       duration of this capture.
* `idle-no-bms.asc`                   — same key state, no charger,
                                       no faults; the clean baseline.

The diff between these two captures localized the fault encoding to
F108 and is what enabled the byte 7 decode. See the F108F3 section.

A subsequent capture extends F108 evidence to bytes outside byte 7:

* `bms-fullcharge-102-109-140.asc` — captured at 100 % SOC with the
                                      dashboard cycling through codes
                                      102 (Total voltage too high),
                                      109 (Battery over voltage), and
                                      140 (System fault level). F108
                                      payload `10 00 04 00 00 00 00 01`
                                      throughout. byte 7 = 0x01 sets
                                      only bit 0 (= 124, the always-on
                                      Clock fault baseline); 140 is NOT
                                      asserted in byte 7 in this
                                      snapshot, consistent with the
                                      dashboard *cycling* through codes
                                      so not all listed codes are
                                      simultaneously active in the
                                      bitmap. **bytes 0 = 0x10 and byte
                                      2 = 0x04 are nonzero for the
                                      first time in any capture**, so
                                      F108 carries fault info beyond
                                      byte 7. The bit-to-code mapping
                                      for those bytes is not yet
                                      established. See the F108F3
                                      section.

Side-by-side toggle captures (top-level, 2026-05-09) document attempts
to find body-control signals on this bus:

* `fwd-on-and-off.asc`     — 4WD selector toggled mid-recording.
* `pto-off-on.asc`         — PTO toggled on at ~t=5 s.
* `pto-on-off.asc`         — PTO toggled off at ~t=5 s.
* `headlights-off-on.asc`  — headlights toggled on at ~t=5 s.

Outcome: **none of the 4WD / PTO / headlight switch *states* appear
on this bus as a discrete flag.** Every byte was checked across all 62
broadcast IDs and no byte transitions cleanly with any of those toggles.
What headlights and PTO *do* produce is a clean step in F100F3 pack
current (the auxiliary load shifts the BMS-reported pack draw); 4WD
selection produces no visible signature at all. This is consistent with
those switches being either hard-wired discrete I/O or carried on a
second CAN segment we don't have a tap on. See "Open questions" for
the two-bus hypothesis.

PGN decode notes (BMS, SA = 0xF3)
---------------------------------

All scalings below were derived empirically. Where a scaling is consistent
with sane physical units across multiple captures it's marked CONFIRMED;
otherwise it's TENTATIVE.

F113..F13C — "BMS cell voltages N..N+3"
    8 bytes = 4 × big-endian uint16 in millivolts.
    F113 = cells 0..3, F114 = cells 4..7, ... F13C = cells 164..167.
    Only the first 20 cells of F113..F117 have nonzero / sensible
    values for this pack; the rest are 0xFFFF (sentinel) or 0.
    CONFIRMED: BE u16 mV. Cells read ~3.6–3.7 V at ~40% SOC.

F155..F15E — "BMS module temps 8N..8N+7"
    8 bytes = 8 × uint8 with J1939 +40 °C offset (i.e. raw 53 = 13 °C).
    F155 = channels 0..7, F156 = 8..15, ... F15E = 72..79.
    Most channels are 0xFF (not present); a small number reflect ambient
    and were what let us pin down the +40 °C convention.
    CONFIRMED: u8, offset 40 °C.

F102F3 — "BMS cell min/max summary"
    Carries pack-wide cell statistics. We use:
      * bytes 1..2 (BE u16) = max-cell mV
      * bytes 3..4 (BE u16) = min-cell mV
      * byte 5            = max-cell **number, 1-based** (CONFIRMED)
      * byte 6            = min-cell **number, 1-based** (CONFIRMED)
      * byte 8            = status/flag bits
      * spread = max - min (mV) — derived
      * a coarse pack-voltage estimate (sum of 20 × cell average), kept
        as a sanity check
    CONFIRMED for max/min/spread.

    Indexing convention (resolved by row-order correlation between F102
    frames and surrounding F113..F117 cell-voltage frames in
    recorded-data/charging.csv):

      * byte5/byte6 are **1-based** cell numbers as the BMS GUI displays
        them (e.g. "Max cell #19").
      * To map to the parser's 0-based `cell_index` in cells.csv,
        subtract 1: BMS cell N = decoder cell_index N-1.
      * Cross-check: in the smallest-spread frames (3 mV) of
        recorded-data/charging.csv, F102 reports byte5=16, byte6=1,
        max=3649 mV, min=3646 mV. The contemporaneous per-cell snapshot
        shows decoder cell_index 15 at 3649 mV (= BMS cell #16 ✓) and
        decoder cell_index 0 as the lowest at 3645 mV (= BMS cell #1 ✓).
      * The vendor screenshot's "Max cell #19 / Min cell #2" therefore
        corresponds to decoder cell_index 18 and cell_index 1.

    Minor caveat: F102's reported min_mv is occasionally 1 mV higher
    than the actual lowest voltage seen in the per-cell PGN snapshot
    around it (e.g. 3646 vs 3645). Two plausible explanations: brief
    timing skew between the F102 summary computation and the per-cell
    sweep, or light filtering inside the BMS. The *index* still
    correctly points at the right cell, just with a slightly stale mV.

    Tie-breaking observation: when several cells are tied at the max,
    the BMS appears to report the lowest-index winner. Across 124
    smallest-spread frames where decoder cell_index {15, 17, 18} were
    all tied at 3649 mV, F102 byte5 was always 16 (= cell #16, i.e.
    decoder index 15, the lowest of the tied set).

    Smallest spread observed across all captures: **3 mV** (124 frames,
    all in recorded-data/charging.csv).

F100F3 — "BMS pack status" (CONFIRMED pack-current source)
    Across all asc/*.asc captures (5 in-neutral + driving.asc with real
    load, plus loader-arms.asc / loader2.asc which exercise hydraulic
    load with the motor controller silent) the byte structure is:

      byte 1 (data[0]): 0x03                  — constant
      byte 2 (data[1]): pack terminal voltage — CONFIRMED.
                                                 V = b * 0.1 + 76.8.
                                                 Range observed across
                                                 all captures: 0x35..0x42
                                                 → 82.1..83.4 V. Drops
                                                 INVERSELY with load
                                                 (sag under driving /
                                                 hydraulic / inverter
                                                 contactor draw).
                                                 Anchored by linear
                                                 regression vs
                                                 20*mean(cell mV)
                                                 across 24 captures
                                                 (residuals < 0.55 V)
                                                 and cross-checked
                                                 against the FF50
                                                 charger frame which
                                                 uses an identical
                                                 encoding (R^2 = 0.986
                                                 across 2863 active-
                                                 charging frames in
                                                 charging-120V-90ish-
                                                 to-100.asc).
      bytes 3-4 (data[2..3]):                 — SIGNED PACK CURRENT,
                                                 16-bit big-endian, 0.1
                                                 A/bit, biased so that
                                                 raw 0x7D00 = 0 A.
                                                 CONFIRMED — see below.
      byte 5 (data[4]): 0xF8..0xFA (248..250) — near-constant. Plausibly
                                                 a current limit / status
                                                 byte; further decoded
                                                 below as TENTATIVE.
      byte 6 (data[5]): 0xFA                  — constant. = 250 raw.
      byte 7 (data[6]): 0x14                  — constant. = 20, the
                                                 SERIES CELL COUNT.
                                                 Cross-check on topology.
      byte 8 (data[7]): 0x00                  — constant.

    Pack current — CONFIRMED:

      pack_current_A = (be16(data[2], data[3]) - 0x7D00) * 0.1

      i.e. the field is signed pack current as a 16-bit big-endian
      value with a fixed bias of 0x7D00 (=32000 raw) and 0.1 A/bit.
      Convention: positive = drawing from pack, negative = charging
      into pack.

      How the earlier "byte 4 is NOT pack current" reading went
      wrong: at idle data[2] sits at 0x7D, so the bias cancels and
      data[3] alone happened to read out as 0..25.5 A — which
      matched the dashboard at rest by coincidence. The moment real
      current crossed ~25.6 A, data[2] ticked to 0x7E and data[3]
      rolled back near zero, making the byte-4-only decode appear
      to drop ("saturate") under load. data[2] = 0x7F is reached in
      driving.asc (peak ≈ 63.6 A under this decode, vs the operator-
      reported dashboard peak of "72" — within sensor / sampling-
      window error).

      Empirical fit:
        * loader-arms.asc idle:        0x7DC4 → +19.6 A   (matches
                                                            dashboard at
                                                            rest)
        * loader-arms.asc raising arm: 0x7E70 → +36.8 A   (matches
                                                            dashboard ~34 A
                                                            during
                                                            lowering)
        * driving.asc peak:            0x7F7C → +63.6 A   (≈ operator-
                                                            reported 72 A)
        * loader-arms.asc / loader2.asc data[2] never drops below 0x7D
          while load was hydraulic-only, so no sign change is needed
          in those captures. driving.asc also stays non-negative
          (regen, if any, is small).

      Dashboard-confirmed calibration set (amp-*.asc, 2026-05-09).
      Each capture is steady-state at a single dashboard-displayed
      amperage; mean decoded current matches the displayed value to
      within ~1 A across the full 0–60 A range. This is the cleanest
      cross-validation of the encoding to date:

        | file          | data[2..3] range  | mean decoded A | dash A |
        |---------------|-------------------|----------------|--------|
        | amp-1.asc     | 0x7D12 (constant) |   1.8          |   1    |
        | amp-18.asc    | 0x7D9D – 0x7DC5   |  17.6          |  18    |
        | amp-35.asc    | 0x7E53 – 0x7E94   |  37.0          |  35    |
        | amp-42.asc    | 0x7E8E – 0x7EC5   |  41.7          |  42    |
        | amp-58.asc    | 0x7F32 – 0x8061   |  62.1          |  58    |

      Notes on this set:
        * amp-1.asc is the only "true idle" snapshot we have on this
          bus — tractor in neutral, key on, no load. data[2..3] is
          *constant* at 0x7D12 across the whole capture (1.8 A
          standby draw for BMS / dashboard / DC-DC). This is much
          lower than the ~17 A seen in earlier "idle in DRIVE"
          captures (drive-fwd, drive-twd, headlights-off-on first
          5 s), confirming that putting the tractor in DRIVE
          energizes inverter / contactor circuitry that pulls
          ~16 A above pure standby.
        * amp-58.asc is the highest-current capture in the set and
          crosses the 0x7F → 0x80 high-byte boundary, exercising
          the second high-byte rollover and confirming the encoding
          extends correctly past 76.7 A.
        * The amp-N "N" in each filename is the operator's read
          off the dashboard at the time of recording, intended as
          rounded ground truth — not a precise calibration marker.
          Sub-amp discrepancies between mean decoded A and dash A
          are expected from rounding / averaging.

      The earlier hypothesis that the dashboard's current came from
      FF21CA byte 2 was wrong — that byte is part of the RPM low
      half (see FF21CA decode section). The operator-confirmed
      reconciliation moment was the loader test on 2026-05-09:
      raising/lowering the loader hydraulics (motor controller
      silent, so FF21CA can't be the source) clearly drives a
      varying current on the bus, and the only signal that tracks
      it is F100F3 bytes 3-4 BE.

    Bytes 5..6 (~0xFA = 250 throughout): plausibly a BMS-published
      flag / limit field. The transient drop to 0xF8 in some
      loader-arms frames hints at a status bit but is not yet
      decoded. TENTATIVE.

    Byte 7 = 0x14 (20): matches the series cell count exactly. Either
      the BMS broadcasts cell count as a sanity field here, or it
      coincidentally tracks. Across all captures it never changes.

F104F3 — "BMS temp min/max summary"
    Pack-wide hottest/coldest module-temperature summary, analogous to
    F102 for cells. Not currently parsed into its own CSV.

F106F3 — "BMS state (charger-dependent)"
    Periodic frame whose contents differ between idle, driving, and
    charging captures. Likely BMS state machine + flags. Across the
    asc/*.asc captures (all 100% SOC, all neutral):
      * driving captures (full-throttle, ft+hyd, accel/decel):
          b0=0x45  b1=0xE0  b2=0xFC  b3..b7=0xFF
      * ignition-with-charger-inserted:
          b0=0x80  b1=0xC4  b2=0xFC  b3..b7=0xFF
      * normal-ignition (during wake-up): b0 transitions through
          0x00, 0x80, 0x45 over the boot sequence.
    Strong "BMS-side running mode" candidate — pairs with the F100D0
    byte-0 mode field on the VC side. Vocabulary so far:
      * 0x00 → init/boot
      * 0x80 → standby / charger-detected
      * 0x45 → ready / driving
    More states (Calibrating, Charging, Discharging, Fault, Sleep)
    expected per the vendor GUI. TENTATIVE.

F107F3 — "BMS current/voltage limits"
    Standard J1939 BMS "limits" frame layout (charge current limit,
    discharge current limit, voltage limits). Now with byte-level
    state evidence from asc/*.asc:
      * driving:          38 A4 27 10 01 76 00 00
      * charger-inserted: 27 10 27 10 00 00 00 00
    Observations:
      * bytes 2..3 = 0x2710 = 10000 in EVERY capture — almost certainly
        a J1939 "not-available" sentinel (10000 in 0.1 A/bit units is
        suspicious; 0xFFFF would be the more conventional sentinel).
      * bytes 0..1 are 0x2710 (= 0 effective limit) when charger is
        inserted but vehicle isn't moving, and 0x38A4 (= 14500) when
        driving. A current-limit field that goes from "no current
        allowed" to "1450 A allowed" would make sense for a high-power
        traction LSB; 0.1 A/bit gives 0 → 1450 A which matches the
        300 A pack rating with margin. TENTATIVE.
      * bytes 4..5 are 0x0176 = 374 driving, 0x0000 charger-inserted.
        374 × 0.2 V/bit = 74.8 V, very close to the rated nominal
        72 V — plausibly a voltage limit / HV cutoff. TENTATIVE.
    Confirming this requires a charge capture from low SOC where the
    BMS will publish meaningful charge-current limits.

F108F3 — "BMS active fault bitmap" (CONFIRMED for byte 7; TENTATIVE elsewhere)
    Active BMS fault flags. All bytes are 0x00 in healthy idle, verified
    against asc/bms-error-codes/idle-no-bms.asc (key on, neutral, no
    charger). Byte 7 is the dashboard-visible BMS warning code bitmap;
    the eight bits map to the vendor "BMS error code" table from the
    operator manual, packed in numeric order with reserved-number bits
    (141) sitting in their natural slot:

        bit 0 (mask 0x01) = code 124  Clock fault
        bit 1 (mask 0x02) = code 140  System fault level
        bit 2 (mask 0x04) = code 141  reserved (no code 141 in manual)
        bit 3 (mask 0x08) = code 142  BMS fault need maintenance
        bit 4 (mask 0x10) = code 143  Battery fault need maintenance
        bit 5 (mask 0x20) = code 144  Battery system fault needs maintenance
        bit 6 (mask 0x40) = code 145  Full charge/discharge cycle needed
        bit 7 (mask 0x80) = code 146  Maintenance mode status

    Cross-validated against asc/bms-error-codes/
    bms-124-140-142-143-144-146.asc, in which the operator confirmed
    the dashboard was cycling through codes 124, 140, 142, 143, 144,
    146 (each held on screen for ~1 s). F108 in that capture is

        00 00 00 00 00 01 00 BB

    throughout. 0xBB = 0b1011_1011 has exactly six bits set (0, 1, 3,
    4, 5, 7), and those six bits are exactly the displayed codes under
    the mapping above. The two zero bits (2, 6) correspond to the two
    inactive codes in this group (141 reserved, 145 not currently
    active).

    Byte 5 takes value 0x01 in `bms-124-140-142-143-144-146.asc` and
    0x00 in healthy idle AND in `bms-fullcharge-102-109-140.asc` (which
    also has active faults, so byte 5 is NOT a universal master-fault
    flag). It correlates with byte 7 having content beyond the always-
    on bit 0 (= 124). Best current guess: byte 5 is a "byte-7 group
    has any non-baseline code asserted" summary flag, or a related
    severity bit. TENTATIVE.

    Bytes 0..6 — additional fault info CONFIRMED (mapping unknown).
    `bms-fullcharge-102-109-140.asc` (operator-confirmed dashboard
    cycling through codes 102, 109, 140) shows F108 =

        10 00 04 00 00 00 00 01

    so byte 0 = 0x10 (bit 4 set) and byte 2 = 0x04 (bit 2 set), both
    nonzero for the first time. byte 7 = 0x01 sets only bit 0 (= 124,
    the always-on baseline) — code 140 is *not* asserted in byte 7
    in this snapshot, which is consistent with the dashboard cycling
    through codes (only some are simultaneously asserted in the
    bitmap at any instant; the "operator-saw-these" filename is a
    union over the cycling, not a state of the snapshot).

    Mapping for bytes 0..6 not yet established. Full BMS error code
    table (per operator manual; see "Vendor BMS error code table"
    section below):

        100..113   misc voltage / current / temperature / unbalance
                    (14 codes; 114-115 reserved)
        116..123   detection / monitoring faults (8 codes)
        124..127   clock / CAN / insulation faults
        140        system fault level
        142..146   maintenance codes (141 reserved)

    32 codes total; F108 has 64 bits, so the layout has plenty of
    room for any choice of grouping. Plausible layouts:

      * "8 codes per byte by category": each byte covers a coherent
        subset (voltage codes / temp codes / detection codes / etc).
        byte 7 already fits this — codes 124 + 140-146 are the
        "system / maintenance" group.
      * "8 codes per byte, sequential, with reserved gaps": byte 0
        = 100-107, byte 1 = 108-113 + reserved, byte 2 = 116-123,
        etc. Doesn't fit byte 7 (which jumps 124 → 140) and doesn't
        cleanly explain byte 0 bit 4 + byte 2 bit 2 with cycling
        codes {102, 109, 140}.
      * Some other vendor-specific grouping that isn't apparent
        from the manual's numeric order.

    Pinning down the bytes-0..6 mapping needs single-code captures
    (one displayed dashboard code per capture) — the byte-7 decode
    only worked because the popcount and code count matched. With
    a single set bit per capture, the layout is unambiguous.

    Earlier interpretation (now superseded): the asc/*.asc captures
    that didn't include any active codes had F108 byte 7 = 0x01
    (driving) and 0xB9 (ignition-with-charger-inserted), which had
    been guessed to be a charger-detected / contactor-state flag.
    Under the now-confirmed mapping, those readings instead translate
    to "code 124 active" and "codes 124, 142, 143, 144, 146 active",
    respectively — consistent with a tractor that always annunciates
    code 124 (Clock fault) once it has been on the bus for any
    length of time, and additionally surfaces several maintenance
    codes when the charger plug is inserted while at 100 % SOC.


PGN decode notes (charger, SA = 0xE5)
-------------------------------------

FF50E5 — "Charger telemetry (V, A)"
    Proprietary B frame from the on-board charger. Byte numbering below
    is 1-based with explicit data[N] (0-based) annotations, matching
    the convention used in the F100F3, F102F3, F108F3, and FF21CA
    sections above.
      * byte 1 (data[0]) = charger status / mode:
          0x00 = idle (not delivering)
          0x01, 0x02 = transient handshake states (only seen briefly
                        during wake-up / ramp; treat as not-delivering)
          0x03 = ACTIVELY delivering charge (CONFIRMED)
      * bytes 2..3 (data[1..2]) LE u16 = output voltage. Same encoding
        as F100F3 byte 2 (data[1]): V = raw * 0.1 + 76.8. CONFIRMED.
      * bytes 4..5 (data[3..4]) LE u16 = output current at 0.1 A/bit
        (no offset). CONFIRMED.

    Status decode CONFIRMED from charging-120V-90ish-to-100.asc — a
    real charging trace from ~90 % SOC with the charger on 120 V mains.
    Status sat at 0x03 for 2863/3007 frames (the active-delivery
    portion); data[1..2] and data[3..4] are only meaningful while
    status == 0x03. The voltage and current scales were anchored
    against contemporaneous F100F3 readings using time-aligned
    regression across those 2863 active-charging frames:
      * data[1..2] vs Pack_V: slope 0.1024, intercept 77.04, R^2 = 0.9856
        → factor 0.1, offset 76.8 V (identical to F100F3 byte 2 / data[1]).
      * data[3..4] vs |Pack_I|: slope 0.0989, intercept -0.07, R^2 = 0.9985
        → 0.1 A/bit, no offset.
    End-to-end the decoded current showed a textbook CC→CV taper
    (~18.5 A → ~9.9 A → ~2.9 A) at constant ~83 V over the capture.

    The earlier "raw 0x00DC ≈ 73.3 V via 1/3 V/bit" hypothesis was
    WRONG — that decoder predates the charging trace and the regression
    against F100. With the corrected formula, raw values seen at idle /
    handshake are not voltage readings at all (see refinement below).

    Refinement from ignition-with-charger-inserted.asc: with the
    charger plug physically inserted in the tractor's port BUT no AC
    mains supplied to the charger, FF50E5 still beacons at ~10 Hz
    (27 frames over 27 s) with status = 0x00, v_raw = 2 and
    i_raw = 2048 (constant). Separately, in normal-ignition.asc
    (no charger connected at all) the charger module ALSO beacons
    during wake-up, with status briefly cycling 0x00 → 0x01 → 0x02
    and v_raw / i_raw sweeping nonsensical values for a few frames.
    These are charger-module self-test artifacts, NOT real bus
    readings — they will inflate the parser summary's V/I range
    unless filtered by status. So:
      * status = 0x00 means "not actively delivering" — does NOT
        distinguish "charger absent" from "charger present but not
        powered" from "charger present, AC live, but BMS-inhibited
        at 100% SOC". Charger-presence detection therefore lives
        elsewhere (current best candidate: F108F3 byte 7).
      * status 0x01/0x02 are transient handshake states. Treat as
        not-delivering for power accounting.
      * Don't trust V/I unless status == 0x03.


PGN decode notes (motor controller, SA = 0xCA)
----------------------------------------------

FF21CA — "Motor telemetry (RPM, throttle, drive state, ctrl temp)"
    Broadcast at ~85 Hz from SA 0xCA. The full 29-bit ID is
    0x0CFF21CA (priority 3), not 0x18FF21CA (priority 6) as one
    might assume from the "default" J1939 broadcast priority — this
    frame ships at a higher priority than the BMS broadcasts, which
    makes sense for a real-time inverter feed. Across the asc/*.asc
    captures (all in neutral) the structure resolves as:

      byte 1 (data[0]): 0x00..0x34            — THROTTLE PEDAL POSITION
                                                 (raw). 0 = released, ~52
                                                 = full pedal. Idle keeps
                                                 byte ~0; full-throttle
                                                 captures sit 29..36;
                                                 accel-decel sweeps 1..45
                                                 leading the RPM ramp.
      byte 2 (data[1]): 0x00                  — constant.
      bytes 3..4 (data[2..3]):                — MOTOR RPM as a 16-bit
                                                 little-endian uint with
                                                 a 0x0C80 (= 3200) bias.
                                                 CONFIRMED — see below.
      byte 5 (data[4]): 0x28 / 0x3B / 0x3C    — three-state field;
                                                 changes near startup
                                                 calibration boundary.
                                                 Not decoded.
      byte 6 (data[5]): 0x35..0x36            — controller temperature
                                                 with the J1939 +40 °C
                                                 offset (53 → 13 °C,
                                                 54 → 14 °C). Near
                                                 ambient throughout.
      byte 7 (data[6]): 0x00                  — constant.
      byte 8 (data[7]): directional pedal selector.
                                                 0x10 (16) = idle / neither
                                                              pedal pressed.
                                                 0x14 (20) = forward pedal
                                                              pressed.
                                                 0x18 (24) = reverse pedal
                                                              pressed.
                                                 CONFIRMED — see "Direction
                                                 encoding" below.

    RPM — CONFIRMED:

      rpm = ((data[3] << 8) | data[2]) - 0x0C80
      Equivalently: rpm = (data[3] - 12) * 256 + (data[2] - 128).

      Empirical fit:
        * Idle (motor commanded zero):
            data[2] = 0x80, data[3] = 0x0C → 0 RPM.
        * Full throttle (pedal pegged, in neutral):
            data[2] sweeps 0x30..0x40 with data[3] = 0x16 →
            ~2480..2496 RPM. Operator reports the dashboard's RPM
            gauge sat near 2500 at this moment, matching to within
            quantization.
        * Accelerate-decelerate (varying throttle, in neutral):
            time-resolved trace shows a textbook ramp from 0 RPM,
            through the full 0..2500 range over ~13 s of acceleration,
            steady cruise at ~2488 RPM, then a symmetric ramp back
            down to 0 RPM. data[3] (the high byte) advances cleanly
            at every multiple-of-256 RPM boundary, exactly as a 16-bit
            value should.

      The 0x0C80 bias is the controller's "zero RPM" sentinel.
      In practice the field appears to be **magnitude only** — see
      "Direction encoding" below — so values below 0x0C80 (which would
      represent negative / reverse rotation under an offset-binary
      reading) are not actually emitted, even when the operator
      drives in reverse. Reverse direction is signaled instead by
      data[7]; the 0x0C80 offset is best understood as a fixed
      configuration constant rather than a true signed-value zero.

    Direction encoding — CONFIRMED:

      data[7] selects the directional pedal that the operator is
      pressing. The Solectrac e25 has a dual-pedal "shuttle" layout —
      one pedal for forward, one for reverse — and FF21CA byte 8
      reports which (if any) is currently depressed:

          0x10 (16)  idle / neither pedal pressed
          0x14 (20)  forward pedal pressed
          0x18 (24)  reverse pedal pressed

      Verified against driving.asc, where the operator reported
      backing out of a parking spot before pulling forward. The
      capture has exactly three transitions:

          t=  1.08 s   data[7] = 0x10 (idle)        boot complete
          t= 15.93 s   0x10 → 0x18 (REVERSE pedal)  back-out begins
          t= 55.29 s   0x18 → 0x10 (idle)           reverse pedal released
          t= 55.79 s   0x10 → 0x14 (FORWARD pedal)  forward driving

      Phase statistics across the full capture match the maneuver:
        idle (b7=0x10): RPM ≈ 0,   throttle ≈ 0..3 except at the
                       brief pedal-swap moment around t=55.3 s.
        reverse (0x18): RPM 0..1369, throttle up to 72; lower top
                       speed consistent with creeping out of the
                       parking spot.
        forward (0x14): RPM 0..2529, throttle up to 82; full
                       sustained driving.

      data[2..3] LE - 0x0C80 stays non-negative throughout — the
      motor controller reports rotational *magnitude*, with sign
      determined separately by data[7]. Treat the decoded RPM as
      "speed" and form a signed value as `direction × |rpm|` if
      direction-aware analysis is needed.

    Frame suppression: FF21CA does not appear at all in
    ignition-with-charger-inserted.asc. Motor controller goes silent
    when the contactors aren't closed for traction.

    Reconciliation with earlier "data[2] = signed pack current"
    interpretation:

      An earlier reading of these notes interpreted data[2] alone as
      a signed pack-current field with 128 = 0, 1 A/bit, citing the
      cross-check that data[2] = 57 → signed -71 ≈ a dashboard
      reading of "72". That interpretation does not survive the
      operator-confirmed RPM = 2500 at full throttle:

        * At full throttle in neutral data[2] sweeps 0x30..0x40 (48..64).
          Under "data[2] = signed current" that's -64..-80 A. An
          unloaded motor at ~2500 RPM in neutral does not draw 70+
          amps; the running friction of an electric drive is on the
          order of single-digit amps. The signal magnitude doesn't
          fit a current reading under this load condition.
        * data[3] varies systematically with throttle (0x0C at idle
          → 0x16 at full throttle). Under "data[3] = state /
          sub-counter" the smooth ramp has no clean explanation.
          Under "RPM high byte" it's exactly the carry from
          data[2] crossing 256-boundaries.
        * data[2..3] LE - 3200 reproduces 0 RPM at idle and ≈2488
          at full throttle to within quantization. Both the
          single-byte current reading AND the RPM reading would
          make data[2] sweep the full range during accel-decel,
          but only the RPM reading explains data[3] simultaneously.

      The previously claimed cross-check (data[2] = 57, dashboard
      "72") was a coincidence: FF21CA data[2] is just the RPM low
      byte. The dashboard's actual pack-current source on this bus
      is F100F3 bytes 3-4 BE (see "Pack current — CONFIRMED" in the
      F100F3 section above). The 2026-05-09 loader-arms test
      resolves this — hydraulic load with the motor controller
      silent showed a clean varying current on F100F3 only, and
      the byte-3-only decode misbehaved exactly as predicted by
      the 16-bit BE biased encoding.

DM1 (FECA) — empty throughout
    Payload `00 00 00 00 00 00 FF FF` in every capture. The motor
    controller is advertising "no active diagnostic trouble codes"
    via the SAE J1939 standard channel even when the dashboard is
    showing numeric error codes. See "Dashboard error codes" below.

--- Legacy section (pre-2026-05-09) ----------------------------------

Operator reports that the tractor's display showed numeric BMS error
codes 102, 109, and 140 during most of the asc/*.asc recording session
(all of which were taken at 100% SOC). A targeted byte-level search
across the 5 captures finds the literal values 0x66, 0x6D, 0x8C in
exactly three places, none of which is an error-code stream:

* F100F3 byte 4 (the pack-current low byte): sweeps the full
  0x00..0xFF range during normal operation as the 16-bit BE current
  field crosses 256-step boundaries, so 102/109/140 appear as
  ordinary mid-range readings. Coincidental.

* FF50E5 byte 1 in normal-ignition.asc: a single-frame hit on 0x8C
  during the charger-module self-test sweep at wake-up. Coincidental.

* FF21CA data[2] (the RPM low byte — see FF21CA decode section above):
  the literal byte values 0x66 (102), 0x6D (109), 0x8C (140) appear
  here as ordinary low bytes of in-range RPM values during normal
  driving (since data[2] sweeps 0..255 every time RPM crosses a
  256-step boundary, every byte value 0x00..0xFF is hit
  routinely). Coincidental.

What 0xCA *is* broadcasting on the standard diagnostic channel:

* DM1 (PGN FECA from SA 0xCA) is being transmitted continuously, but
  with payload `00 00 00 00 00 00 FF FF` — the SAE J1939-encoded
  representation of "no active diagnostic trouble codes". That is,
  while the dashboard is displaying error codes, the BMS is *also*
  advertising via the standard channel that it has no active codes.
  These two facts are not consistent under any straightforward model
  where the BMS is the source of truth for the codes.

Most likely explanations, in rough order of plausibility:

1. **The codes are dashboard-synthesized, not BMS-broadcast.** The
   dashboard sees the same FF21CA / F1xx telemetry we do and
   independently computes "code 102 / 109 / 140" based on local
   thresholds (e.g., a 100%-SOC + voltage-band combination). The BMS
   itself doesn't know there's a "code"; that's why DM1 is empty.
   This would mean the codes will never appear on the bus regardless
   of what we capture passively.

2. **The codes live on the un-tapped drivetrain bus.** The dashboard
   is almost certainly a gateway between this bus and a second CAN
   segment we haven't tapped yet. The codes might be broadcast there
   (by the motor controller, traction inverter, or another ECU).

3. **The codes are stored in the BMS but only reported by request.**
   Standard J1939 has DM2 (previously-active DTCs, broadcast) and
   DM4/DM7 (request/response for specific DTCs). None of those are
   present in the asc captures because no client is querying them.
   The dashboard might issue these requests internally on a tap point
   we don't see, or via a vendor UDS/KWP2000 service we'd have to
   trigger explicitly.

4. **The codes are encoded in a multi-byte field of an existing
   broadcast PGN that we haven't decoded.** Possible candidates:
   F104F3 (constant payload `3A 38 06 01 02 FF FF FF` — the leading
   `3A 38` ≈ 58 / 56 doesn't match the reported codes, but the field
   semantics are unknown), or F108F3 byte 7 (`0x01` normal,
   `0xB9` with charger inserted — 0xB9 = 185, not a reported code,
   but this byte clearly carries flag bits we haven't decoded). None
   of these PGNs *literally contain* 102/109/140, but a bit-mask
   interpretation could in principle decode to those ordinal IDs.

To pin this down, the next capture should ideally:

* Be triggered specifically while one of the codes is on the display
  (not just "during a session where they appeared at some point").
* Cover an explicit clear/cycle of the code on the dashboard (key off
  or whatever the operator menu offers) so we can see what frame
  changes contemporaneously.
* Optionally, send DM2 / DM7 request frames from the logger
  (1806CAxx with the right payload) and capture any responses. This
  is active, not passive, so it's a different kind of test.

Until then: status of the codes' on-bus encoding is **UNKNOWN**, and
the dashboard's display of them does NOT prove the BMS broadcasts
them.

Motor-controller error codes — methodology to decode (open)
-----------------------------------------------------------

We do not yet have a fault-mode capture for the motor controller (SA 0xCA).
DM1 (FECA) is empty in every existing capture — same situation we had
for the BMS before F108 was decoded — so the standard J1939 trouble-code
channel is not the source. Methodology by analogy with the BMS work:

What's already on the bus from SA 0xCA:
* FECA (DM1)  — empty (`00 00 00 00 00 00 FF FF`).
* FF21CA      — motor telemetry (~85 Hz). Three bytes are still
                undecoded and are the highest-value MC-fault candidates:
                  * byte 2 (data[1]): 0x00 constant in all captures
                  * byte 5 (data[4]): three-state 0x28 / 0x3B / 0x3C
                                      (changes near startup calibration)
                  * byte 7 (data[6]): 0x00 constant in all captures
                A constant-zero byte that "lights up" only under fault
                conditions is exactly the F108-byte-7 pattern. byte 7 is
                the strongest single candidate; byte 2 next; byte 5 is
                more likely a state-machine field than a fault bitmap
                (it changes during normal startup, which a fault flag
                shouldn't).
* Possibly a separate proprietary PGN we haven't seen because it's
  silent until a fault asserts. Worth enumerating *all* (PGN, SA=0xCA)
  pairs across every existing asc/*.asc to confirm FECA + FF21CA is
  really all 0xCA emits in the no-fault state.
* A capture might also surface MC fault info via a non-0xCA proxy
  (the dashboard or VC re-broadcasting); enumerate any *new* PGNs
  that appear in a fault capture but not in any clean baseline.

Capture procedure (mirrors the BMS pair that nailed down F108):

1. Clean baseline in the same key state as the planned fault capture.
   Key on, in DRIVE (not neutral — the MC has more state machinery
   awake in DRIVE; FF21CA byte 5 shifts), no faults active. Call it
   `mc-clean-drive.asc`.
2. Fault capture with one (ideally just one) MC code on the dashboard.
   Single-code is critical: with the BMS we got lucky (six set bits in
   numeric order) and could deduce the layout from one popcount match.
   With a single set bit, the encoding is unambiguous regardless of
   layout. Operator menu / cycle behavior of the dashboard determines
   how to isolate one code.
3. Byte-level diff between the two captures, restricted to messages
   from SA 0xCA (and any new PGN that appears only in the fault file).
   The change set should be small.
4. Hypothesis check against the operator manual's MC error-code table
   (assuming Solectrac publishes one analogous to the BMS table).
   Popcount of the set bits should equal the count of displayed codes;
   the bit-to-code mapping should fall out of single-code captures.

Things to watch for:

* FF21CA goes silent during charging (contactors open for traction).
  Captures must be in a traction-ready state — fault captures while
  plugged in won't carry FF21CA at all.
* MC error code numbering may not share a numeric range with the BMS
  table. A code 124 on the dashboard could in principle be either
  BMS:124 or MC:124 depending on which subsystem the dashboard is
  annunciating. Confirm against context (which subsystem the manual
  attributes the code to) before reading too much into the number.
* If the diff comes back empty (no FF21CA bytes change, no new PGNs
  from 0xCA appear, no proxy frames change), that's the answer —
  MC error codes aren't on this segment. They're then either:
    - dashboard-synthesized from telemetry (analogous to hypothesis
      #1 in the legacy "Dashboard error codes" section), or
    - on the un-tapped drivetrain bus.
* Don't intentionally induce dangerous faults to get a capture.
  Reasonable MC faults to wait for or trigger safely: motor
  over-temperature from sustained hydraulic-blocked operation,
  low-pack-V cutoff during depleted-pack driving, direction-switch
  contention from rapidly toggling forward/reverse pedals. Avoid
  anything involving disconnecting sensor harnesses or stalling
  the motor against a hard mechanical stop.

Triage order if a fault capture is obtained:

1. Diff FF21CA byte 7 first. Constant-zero across all clean captures
   → highest prior probability of being the fault bitmap.
2. Diff FF21CA byte 2 next. Same reasoning, weaker prior because
   nothing else suggests it carries semantics.
3. Enumerate any new (PGN, SA=0xCA) pairs only present in the fault
   capture.
4. Enumerate any byte in any frame whose value range expanded versus
   the clean baseline.
5. Only after the above turn up nothing should the un-tapped-second-
   bus or dashboard-synthesized hypotheses be advanced; both imply
   passive MC-fault capture on this segment is impossible and the
   work shifts to active probing or a second tap.

Implications for decoding work the screenshot does *not* close out:

* Running-mode vocabulary includes at least "Calibrating" plus the
  parser's current "init" (0x00) and "ready" (0x0C). "Charging",
  "Discharging", "Fault", "Sleep" are likely. The full enumeration of
  F100D0 byte 0 (and probably an analogous BMS-side mode byte in
  F106F3) cannot be filled in from a single snapshot.
* SOC and SOH are not yet decoded from any captured PGN. Candidate
  homes: F104, F106, F107, F108. With SOC=0% and SOH=100% in the
  screenshot, the values are not specific enough to grep for; richer
  states are needed to pin them down.
* Alarms are reported with severity ("lvl 2"), implying a multi-level
  alarm word — likely in F108. Not yet decoded.
* Wake-up signal source ("KL15") implies an ignition-status bit
  somewhere in the BMS broadcasts. Not yet identified.

Decoder errata exposed by the screenshot:

* The **"Cells in parallel: 20"** field on the GUI almost certainly
  refers to parallel groups internal to the pack (20S20P × ~15 Ah cells
  ≈ 300 Ah), not anything visible on CAN. Don't confuse with series
  count.
* The 300 A rated discharge limit was the trigger that exposed the
  earlier mis-decode of pack current as a single uint8 byte. RESOLVED
  on 2026-05-09: pack current is F100F3 bytes 3-4 BE, biased by
  0x7D00, at 0.1 A/bit — 16 bits is enough headroom to encode the
  rated 300 A range comfortably (and signed regen below 0). See the
  F100F3 section above. The 0.1 A/bit LSB stands; what changed is
  that we now read both bytes instead of just the low one.


Source-CSV timestamp column is unusable (recorded-data/*.csv only)
-----------------------------------------------------------------

This section applies only to the original SavvyCAN-style CSVs in
`recorded-data/`. The newer `asc/*.asc` captures (Vector ASC format)
do carry real per-frame microsecond timestamps and are not affected.



The "Time Stamp" column in recorded-data/*.csv is essentially a single
constant value across an entire capture (e.g. `62336000` is shared by
nearly all 70,920 rows of recorded-data/charging.csv, with only sporadic
increments to `62338000`). After parsing, this propagates into the
output CSVs:

    cells.csv         — 8960 rows, 1 unique timestamp
    cell_summary.csv  — 4470 rows, 1 unique timestamp

Consequences:

* Any timestamp-based join between cells / temps / cell_summary / etc.
  is meaningless.
* Time-derivative quantities (dV/dt, dT/dt, frame rate, integrated Ah)
  cannot be computed from these captures.
* The only reliable synchronization between F102 and the surrounding
  F113..F117 cell-voltage stream is **row order within a single source
  CSV**. The F102 ↔ cell-snapshot correlation that established 1-based
  indexing was done that way.

Whatever logger produced these CSVs is either not configured to emit
real per-frame timestamps, or is emitting them in a unit / field the
parser is not consuming. Worth investigating before recording further
captures, since fixing this at the source is the cheapest way to
unlock all time-domain analysis.


Confirmed quantitative findings
-------------------------------

* Resting / standby pack draw (continuous.csv): on the order of
  100–200 W. Consistent with BMS + dash + accessory loads, no traction.
* Driving (driving.csv): peak draw on the order of ~1 kW; typical
  driving current well under nameplate.
* Driving + hydraulics (dph.csv): hydraulic actuation adds clearly
  visible current spikes on top of the drive baseline; peak combined
  draw on the order of ~1.8 kW. The mean of dph.csv is *lower* than
  driving.csv only because dph.csv includes more idle/parked time —
  per-event peaks are higher in dph.
* 100% SOC reference (asc/*.asc): all 5 captures show cell means
  in the 4153–4165 mV band (8–12 mV cell-to-cell within a single
  capture; 6–8 mV F102 spread). Pack-V estimate (20 × cell mean):
    - idle / no charger:       83.29 V
    - charger inserted, no AC: 83.31 V
    - full throttle (neutral): 83.22 V
    - accel/decel (neutral):   83.18 V
    - full thr + hyd (neutral):83.06 V
  The 0.25 V sag from idle to highest load is the cleanest pack-V
  load response we have on this dataset.
* Charging (charging.csv): charger output on the order of ~1.5 kW into
  the pack at this SOC. Pack current reported by F100F3 bytes 3-4 BE
  matches charger current from FF50E5 within rounding (sign convention:
  charging shows up as the raw 16-bit value sitting below 0x7D00),
  which is what cross-validates the 0.1 A/bit scaling.
* Cell spread at rest: tens of mV. No outlier cells observed.
* No active DM1 trouble codes during any capture (FECA frames are
  emitted but their SPN/FMI fields are inactive).
* Temperatures: all populated channels read 12–15 °C, consistent with
  the ~13 °C ambient. No self-heating signature in any capture (none
  of these runs are long or hard enough to warm the pack).


Open questions / future work
----------------------------

* F100 byte 2 voltage / SOC proxy — TENTATIVE. data[1] varies inversely
  with load (0x3B..0x41 across captures) but the dynamic range is too
  small for a clean single-LSB voltage decode. Could be a coarser SOC
  estimate or a low-resolution voltage proxy. Capturing a full charge
  from low SOC to 100% should help pin it down.
* Pack-current sign and magnitude — RESOLVED on 2026-05-09. The
  signal is F100F3 bytes 3-4 BE, biased so raw 0x7D00 = 0 A, at 0.1
  A/bit, signed (positive = drawing, negative = charging). The
  earlier "byte 4 only" decode coincidentally matched at idle because
  data[2] sits at 0x7D and the bias cancels; the moment current
  crossed ~25.6 A the high byte ticked and the low byte rolled,
  producing the "saturating" behavior previously reported. The
  loader-arms.asc / loader2.asc captures (hydraulic load with the
  motor controller silent) ruled out FF21CA as the source and made
  the F100 BE encoding obvious. See the F100F3 section above.
* F106 / F107 / F108 byte-level decoding — partial progress from the
  asc/*.asc captures. F106 byte 0 is the running-mode candidate
  (0x00/0x80/0x45 vocabulary observed). F107 fits the standard J1939
  "limits" frame layout (current/voltage limits, with 0x2710 as a
  sentinel). F108 byte 7 is the charger-detected flag candidate
  (0x01 normal, 0xB9 with charger inserted). All TENTATIVE and need
  charge-from-low-SOC and in-gear-under-load captures to confirm.
* Dashboard error codes (102, 109, 140 ...) — chase down where these
  are coming from. Suggested experiments:
    - Capture again with a single code freshly triggered on the
      display, then key-cycle to clear it; look for any frame whose
      bytes change in lockstep. (Passive; cheap.)
    - Issue J1939 DM2 (previously-active DTCs) and DM7 (request a
      specific DTC) requests from a CAN tool and capture responses.
      DM7 in particular lets you ask the BMS specifically about
      codes 102/109/140. (Active; requires a transmit-capable tool.)
    - If neither shows results, the codes are almost certainly
      either dashboard-synthesized or on the drivetrain bus.
* Drivetrain bus — partially RESOLVED. Motor RPM and throttle ARE on
  this bus, encoded in FF21CA from SA 0xCA (motor controller). So the
  "RPM is on a different segment" hypothesis was wrong. Whether there
  is *also* a separate drivetrain bus carrying inverter / PTO / motor
  current telemetry is still open — none of the asc captures cover an
  in-gear-under-load run, so we don't yet know whether the dashboard's
  current readout is sourced from this bus (in a frame we haven't
  decoded) or from a second segment. If a second tap is investigated,
  plausible locations remain:
    - a separate diagnostic port (look for one near the controller)
    - a 2-pin service tap (small Deutsch connector)
    - a backshell tap on the dash harness
  But this is now a "find what *else* is on a second bus" task rather
  than "find RPM."
* Full-SOC capture — DONE. Five asc/*.asc captures at 100% SOC are
  now in `asc/`. Established cell-voltage band (~4.16 V/cell) and
  pack-V band (~83.2 V) at full SOC; provided the first byte-level
  state evidence on F106 / F107 / F108. The 2026-05-09 loader-arms
  test (hydraulic load, motor silent) closed the F100 pack-current
  decode question — see the F100F3 section.
* Confirm whether dashboard is the bus gateway, or whether a dedicated
  gateway ECU sits between the two segments. Either is plausible.
* Identify the BMS vendor via the Escorts trail. The GUI's
  "ESCORTS-INTERNAL" classification narrows the candidate OEM list
  considerably; locating a protocol PDF would close out most TENTATIVE
  items at once.
* Real per-frame timestamps — DONE for the asc/*.asc batch. Vector
  ASC format carries microsecond-resolution monotonic timestamps,
  so all time-domain analyses (rate, dV/dt, integrated Ah, frame
  pacing) are now possible on those captures. Original recorded-
  data/*.csv files remain unusable for time-derivative analysis.
* Re-validate F100 pack-current scaling — DONE on 2026-05-09 with
  the loader-arms tests. Outcome: bytes 3-4 BE are signed pack
  current at 0.1 A/bit with a 0x7D00 bias. The earlier conclusion
  that "byte 4 saturates" was a mis-read of the high byte ticking
  (0x7D → 0x7E → 0x7F) while only the low byte was being decoded.
  Idle 0x7DC4 → +19.6 A matches dashboard at rest; loader peak
  0x7E70 → +36.8 A matches dashboard ~34 A; driving.asc peak
  0x7F7C → +63.6 A is consistent with the operator-reported ~72 A
  (within sensor / sampling-window error).
* Hunt SOC / SOH / alarms in F104 / F106 / F107 / F108. The vendor
  GUI snapshot gave us a vocabulary (LowSoc lvl 2, Calibrating,
  KL15, etc.) but not enough states to disambiguate byte positions
  from a single snapshot.


Confidence summary
------------------

CONFIRMED:
    cell-voltage scaling (BE u16 mV)
    module-temp scaling (u8 + 40 °C)
    pack topology: 20S, 7 temp probes (vendor GUI)
    pack chemistry: NMC (vendor GUI: "NiCoMn")
    charger output V (FF50 LE u16, V = raw * 0.1 + 76.8; identical
        encoding to F100F3 byte 1; meaningful only while
        FF50 status == 0x03)
    charger output A (FF50 LE u16 @ 0.1 A/bit, no offset; meaningful
        only while FF50 status == 0x03)
    FF50 status byte 0: 0x00 = idle, 0x01/0x02 = transient handshake,
        0x03 = actively delivering charge. CONFIRMED from
        charging-120V-90ish-to-100.asc (2863/3007 frames at 0x03).
    pack voltage at BMS terminals (F100F3 byte 1): V = b * 0.1 + 76.8.
        Anchored by 24-capture regression vs 20*mean(cell mV)
        (residuals < 0.55 V) and confirmed by FF50 active-charging
        regression.
    vehicle-controller heartbeat init/ready states (F100D0 byte 0)
    F102 byte5 = max-cell number, 1-based
    F102 byte6 = min-cell number, 1-based
    pack-V estimate formula (20 × mean cell mV) cross-validates against
        the GUI's displayed pack voltage
    100% SOC reference: ~4.16 V/cell, pack ~83.2 V, F102 spread 6–8 mV
    FF50 status = 0x00 means "not actively delivering charge" — does
        NOT distinguish "charger absent" from "charger inserted but
        unpowered" (charger module beacons either way at low values)
    Vector ASC files carry real per-frame timestamps; recorded-data
        SavvyCAN CSVs do not.
    FF21CA bytes 3..4 (data[2..3]) = motor RPM, little-endian uint16
        with bias 0x0C80. Cross-validated against operator-reported
        ~2500 RPM at full throttle in neutral and a textbook 0→2500→0
        ramp in accellerate-decelerate.asc.
    FF21CA byte 1 (data[0]) = throttle pedal position, raw, full-scale
        ~0x34. Sweeps with operator pedal input and leads the RPM ramp.
    FF21CA byte 6 (data[5]) = motor controller temperature, J1939
        +40 °C offset (53 → 13 °C). Near ambient in all captures.
    FF21CA byte 8 (data[7]) = directional pedal selector:
        0x10 = idle / neither pedal, 0x14 = forward pedal, 0x18 =
        reverse pedal. Verified against driving.asc transitions
        matching the operator's reverse-then-forward maneuver.
    FF21CA RPM is a magnitude (always ≥ 0); reverse is signaled by
        data[7] = 0x18, not by a sub-bias value in data[2..3].
    FF21CA frame is suppressed entirely while charging — motor
        controller goes silent when contactors are open for traction.
    F100F3 bytes 3..4 (data[2..3]) BE = signed pack current,
        biased so raw 0x7D00 = 0 A, scale 0.1 A/bit. Positive =
        drawing from pack, negative = charging. Cross-validated
        against the dashboard at idle (19.6 A on `0x7DC4`), the
        loader-arms test (peak 0x7E70 → +36.8 A vs dashboard ~34
        A), and driving.asc (peak 0x7F7C → +63.6 A vs operator-
        reported ~72 A). Further confirmed by the amp-*.asc
        steady-state set (1, 18, 35, 42, 58 A dashboard) which
        exercises the full 0–60 A range and crosses the 0x7D→0x7E
        and 0x7F→0x80 high-byte boundaries; mean decoded current
        matches dashboard within ~1 A across the set. Resolves the
        prior "byte 4 saturates" confusion — the high byte was
        simply ticking 0x7D → 0x7E → 0x7F as the low byte rolled.
    F108 byte 7 = dashboard-displayed BMS warning code bitmap.
        bit 0=124 (Clock fault), bit 1=140 (System fault level),
        bit 2=141 (reserved), bit 3=142 (BMS needs maint),
        bit 4=143 (Battery needs maint), bit 5=144 (Battery sys
        needs maint), bit 6=145 (Full cycle needed),
        bit 7=146 (Maintenance mode). Cross-validated against
        asc/bms-error-codes/bms-124-140-142-143-144-146.asc
        (operator-confirmed dashboard cycling those 6 codes;
        observed F108 = 00 00 00 00 00 01 00 BB; 0xBB = exactly
        bits {0,1,3,4,5,7} matching {124,140,142,143,144,146})
        and against asc/bms-error-codes/idle-no-bms.asc (no
        faults; F108 all zeros). Vendor-table code numbers from
        the operator manual.

    F100 byte 5 (data[4]) — 0xF8..0xFA. Plausibly a current-limit /
        status flag. Rare drops to 0xF8 under sustained hydraulic

    F100 byte 6 (data[5]) = 0xFA constant — possibly a related

    F100 byte 7 = 0x14 constant: probably the series cell count.
